# Lens: mid-market SaaS

An **emphasis lens**, not a mold. Use it to sharpen probing and proposals when the org resembles a B2B SaaS company (recurring software revenue, named-account selling, a sales/RevOps motion). The universal skeleton is unchanged; this lens tilts *where the weight goes*.

**Center of gravity:** account-level buying intent and the buying committee. Thousands of named accounts, not millions of anonymous people.

**Unit of decision:** which accounts/contacts deserve a touch right now, through which play — including "wait."

**Where identity is hard (L2):** de-anonymizing web visitors and resolving fragmented identifiers into one canonical person at one canonical company; confidence tiers gate whether a signal can drive 1:1 outbound.

**Fact predicates to probe for (L3c):** `in_market`, `intent_score_band`, `budget_status`, `champion_status`, `stage`, `tech_stack_has`, `icp_tier`, `engaged_channel`, `single_threaded`, `renewal_window`.

**Models that usually matter (L5):** ICP fit / tiering, intent/readiness, propensity/conversion (calibrated), expected value (`P(win) × deal_size × strategic_weight`), lookalike, churn/expansion risk.

**Summary stores (L6):** buying-committee, intent, lookalike, enrichment, outcome, and a ~500-token per-account summary for the rep morning brief.

**Hard constraints in the policy layer (L7):** AE/rep capacity, send/inbox limits, ad budget, brand-fatigue caps, suppression/consent, deliverability (warmed domains, spam-rate circuit-breaker).

**Agents that usually matter (L8):** web research, enrichment, buying-committee mapping, scoring, inbound chat, email/LinkedIn copy (assisted → autonomous once precision proven), reply/booking, routing.

**Ground-truth discipline (L9):** delayed labels (did the deal close? was the de-anon right?); shadow-mode the daily brief before autonomy.

**Flagship framing for the Strategy Readout:** "the Brain tells each rep the 5 accounts to focus on today, ranked by expected value, and why."
