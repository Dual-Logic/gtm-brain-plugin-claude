# Lens: professional services

An **emphasis lens**, not a mold. Use it when the org sells expertise through relationships and partner time (consulting, agency, accounting, legal, IT services). The universal skeleton is unchanged; this lens tilts *where the weight goes*.

**Center of gravity:** the **relationship graph** — who knows whom, how well, since when — and the scarcity of partner/senior time.

**Unit of decision:** **bid / no-bid**, who makes the introduction, and which relationships get scarce partner attention — including "wait."

**Where identity is hard (L2):** resolving people/orgs to canonical entities *and quantifying relationship strength* (frequency, recency, reciprocity, seniority) with decay; tracking firm alumni and client-contact job changes as first-class events (the warm-door engine). People move — model employment history; the relationship follows the person, not the domain.

**Fact predicates to probe for (L3c):** `incumbent_provider`, `contract_renewal_window`, `decision_maker`, `procurement_led`, `budget_cycle`, `alumni_at`, `referral_source`, `engagement_health`, `fee_sensitivity`, `conflict_flag`, `single_threaded`.

**Models that usually matter (L5):** ideal-client fit by practice, win probability (calibrated → feeds bid/no-bid), engagement EV (`P(win) × fee × margin × (1 + follow_on) − pursuit_cost`, pursuit cost dominated by partner hours), follow-on/expansion propensity, client attrition risk, referral likelihood. Small-N — prefer simple models + priors + honest intervals.

**Summary stores (L6):** relationship, pursuit, client-health, trigger, outcome/precedent, and a ~500-token per-client/per-pursuit summary for the partner brief.

**Hard constraints in the policy layer (L7):** partner-hour budgets, **conflict-of-interest and independence rules (a blocking, audited gate)**, relationship-fatigue caps, proposal-team capacity, suppression (active-litigation counterparties, procurement blackout).

**Agents that usually matter (L8):** target research, relationship-path (drafts the internal "can you introduce me?"), alumni/people-move watch, RFP qualification, pursuit brief, proposal assist, outreach draft (**client-facing sends stay assisted forever — the relationship owner is the sender of record**), client-health flagging.

**Ground-truth discipline (L9):** structured win/loss debriefs as facts (not slideware); follow-on/referral labels land 6–24 months later; report uncertainty on small N.

**Flagship framing for the Strategy Readout:** "the Brain briefs each partner on their highest-value moves today — which pursuit to bid, which warm door just opened, which key relationship is going cold — and drafts the outreach for them to send."
