# Play Probing Hints — INTERNAL to the interviewer

> **Read this carefully: this is a palette for *your* ears, not the operator's.** It is a list of
> common GTM plays, organized by motion, that the interviewer uses to **probe well** — to ask sharp
> follow-ups and recognize a play when the operator describes one in their own words. It is **never**
> read aloud, pasted, or offered as a menu. Surfacing a catalog to the operator violates R3 (plays are
> *discovered*, not picked). Use these to sharpen your listening, then let the operator's real goals
> name the plays.
>
> **Provenance & refresh.** This palette is authored from common GTM play patterns as a starting
> point. The plugin's on-stage companion, the brainRoadshow demo, showcases a live set of plays; the
> plugin owner should periodically reconcile/augment this file against brainRoadshow's actual
> demonstrated surfaces. Treat it as a refreshable snapshot, not canon.

## How to use it

1. Establish the org's **motion** first (PLG/SaaS, sales-led B2B, relationship/partner-led services,
   DTC/e-commerce, or a hybrid). That narrows which plays are even plausible.
2. Ask about **goals and pains** — "what decisions do you wish were automatic or made better?" Listen
   for a play shape.
3. When the operator describes something that rhymes with a play below, probe deeper on *the decision*
   (what judgment they want the brain to own), not the mechanics.
4. Discover 2–4 **priority** plays. Don't force all of these — most orgs have a handful that matter.

Each entry: **the decision it automates** · *probing angles*.

## PLG / product-led SaaS

- **PQL routing / lead scoring** — which self-serve users/accounts are sales-ready vs. stay
  self-serve. *Probe:* what in-product behavior signals real intent? who decides a trial gets a human?
- **Expansion / upsell detection** — which accounts are ready for a seat/tier expansion, and why.
  *Probe:* what usage or seat pattern precedes expansion today? who acts on it?
- **Churn / contraction early-warning** — which accounts are silently at risk before renewal.
  *Probe:* what's the earliest signal of disengagement? does CS find out in time?
- **Activation / onboarding rescue** — which new accounts are stalling before their aha moment.
  *Probe:* what's the activation milestone? what happens when someone doesn't hit it?

## Sales-led / B2B

- **Inbound lead routing & prioritization** — which inbound leads matter, and who works them first.
  *Probe:* how are leads triaged today? what gets dropped?
- **Account/ICP prioritization** — which target accounts deserve effort now. *Probe:* what makes an
  account "worth it"? is that scored anywhere?
- **Intent-triggered outreach** — which accounts just showed buying intent worth a touch. *Probe:*
  do you have intent signals? what do you do when one fires?
- **Stalled-deal revival** — which stuck/silent opportunities are worth re-opening, and with what
  angle. *Probe:* how do deals go dark? how do you decide which to revive?
- **Pipeline-hygiene / next-best-action** — which open deals need an action this week. *Probe:* how
  do reps decide what to do next?

## Relationship / partner-led (professional services)

- **Relationship-cadence / warm-network nurture** — which valuable relationships are going cold and
  worth a partner touch, with a reason. *Probe:* how do you track relationship health? what falls off?
- **Referral-source activation** — which referral partners to re-engage now. *Probe:* where do your
  best deals originate? do referral sources go quiet?
- **Trigger-event / timing detection** — which target orgs/contacts just had an event (leadership
  change, funding, regulatory shift) that opens a conversation. *Probe:* what event makes a cold
  contact suddenly timely?
- **Proposal / pursuit prioritization** — which opportunities warrant a partner's scarce time.

## DTC / e-commerce

- **High-LTV identification + VIP routing** — which customers are likely high-value early, and what
  treatment they get. *Probe:* what early behavior predicts a great customer?
- **Lapse detection + winback** — which repeat buyers are lapsing, and what offer they warrant.
  *Probe:* what's a normal purchase cadence? when is someone "gone"?
- **Paid-acquisition audience feedback** — which segments to seed (lookalikes) or suppress to raise
  ROAS. *Probe:* do your best-customer segments feed back into ad platforms today?
- **Post-purchase / cross-sell sequencing** — which next product to put in front of whom.

## Cross-motion (can appear anywhere)

- **Data-quality / enrichment triage** — which records are too thin to act on and need enrichment.
- **Segmentation refresh** — keeping "who's who" current as behavior changes.
- **Alert routing** — getting the right signal to the right human at the right moment.

## Reminder

The play's *name* matters less than **the decision it automates** and **the signals that drive it** —
that's what the rest of the interview (architecture + build spec) is built on. Capture plays in the
operator's language; you can label them cleanly later.
